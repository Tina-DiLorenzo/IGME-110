What is a

GAMING ADDICTION?

Gaming addiction was only recently classified as a disorder in May of
2019 by the World Health Organization. This recent classification states
that frequent gaming becomes an addiction when it is, “characterized by
impaired control over gaming, increasing priority given to gaming over other
activities to the extent that gaming takes precedence over other interests
and daily activities, and continuation or escalation of gaming despite the
occurrence of negative consequences.” (World Health Organization 2018).

While games are created to entertain, too much of a good thing can become
a problem. Too much gaming, for example, may lead to gaming addiction. Rather
than for entertainment, players may become unable to tear themselves away from
their computer and may even spend a gratuitous amount of money on in-game
purchases similar to gambling addiction. Game designers incorporate addictive
tactics as a way to keep players invested and to earn more money at the expense
of their player base. Game designers should recognize their obligation to keep
players safe due to the negative consequences of game addictions, the ability to
encourage breaks, and backlash from players.
